DERISE (Desloratadine) — Clinical Trial Summary
=================================================

TRIAL 1: ARIA Study — Efficacy in Seasonal Allergic Rhinitis
-------------------------------------------------------------
Study Design: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, multicenter
Patients: 924 adults with seasonal allergic rhinitis
Duration: 4 weeks
Arms: Desloratadine 5mg/10mg/20mg vs placebo

Key Results:
- Desloratadine 10mg significantly reduced Total Symptom Score (TSS) by 38% vs placebo (p<0.001)
- Desloratadine 20mg reduced TSS by 42% vs placebo (p<0.001)
- Onset of action within 1.25 hours for nasal symptoms
- No significant difference in sedation rates between active arms and placebo
- Patient satisfaction: 78% reported "good" or "excellent" relief with 10mg

TRIAL 2: COMPASS Study — Chronic Urticaria
--------------------------------------------
Study Design: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
Patients: 416 adults with chronic idiopathic urticaria
Duration: 6 weeks
Arms: Desloratadine 10mg vs 20mg vs placebo

Key Results:
- Both doses significantly reduced pruritus severity score vs placebo (p<0.001)
- 20mg showed faster onset of wheal reduction (Day 1 vs Day 2 for 10mg)
- Mean number of hives reduced by 62% (10mg) and 71% (20mg) at Week 6
- Quality of life (DLQI) improved significantly in both treatment groups
- No significant increase in somnolence vs placebo

TRIAL 3: DREAM Study — Drowsiness and Real-world Performance
--------------------------------------------------------------
Study Design: Crossover, double-blind, placebo-controlled
Patients: 200 adults (healthy volunteers)
Duration: 7 days per arm
Arms: Desloratadine 10mg vs cetirizine 10mg vs diphenhydramine 25mg vs placebo

Key Results:
- Desloratadine showed NO significant impairment on driving simulation tests vs placebo
- Cetirizine showed mild impairment in 12% of subjects
- Diphenhydramine (first-gen) showed significant impairment in 45% of subjects
- Desloratadine: Cognitive function tests (digit symbol substitution) — no difference from placebo
- Confirms non-sedating profile suitable for patients who drive or operate machinery

TRIAL 4: Zenrac-DER-001 — Post-Marketing Surveillance (India)
---------------------------------------------------------------
Study Design: Prospective, open-label, observational
Patients: 2,150 Indian adults with allergic rhinitis or urticaria
Duration: 12 weeks
Sites: 85 centers across India

Key Results:
- Overall efficacy rate: 82% of patients rated treatment as "effective" or "very effective"
- Derise 10mg: 76% efficacy in mild allergic rhinitis
- Derise 20mg: 84% efficacy in moderate allergic rhinitis (patients prefer once-daily dosing)
- Derise 50mg: 79% efficacy in severe allergic dermatitis
- Adverse events: 4.2% overall (headache 1.8%, dry mouth 1.1%, fatigue 0.9%, other 0.4%)
- Drowsiness rate: Only 0.7% — significantly less than cetirizine comparator arm (3.1%)
- Patient compliance: 91% adherence to once-daily regimen over 12 weeks
- Patients reporting less drowsiness was a key differentiator vs competitor products

SAFETY SUMMARY ACROSS TRIALS:
- No QTc prolongation observed at doses up to 45mg (4.5x recommended dose)
- No clinically significant changes in liver enzymes
- Weight-neutral — no significant weight gain vs placebo
- No withdrawal effects on discontinuation
- Safe in combination with inhaled corticosteroids and bronchodilators
