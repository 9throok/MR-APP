RILAST (Montelukast) — Prescribing Information
================================================

BRAND NAME: Rilast
GENERIC NAME: Montelukast Sodium
MANUFACTURER: Zenrac Pharmaceuticals Pvt. Ltd.
THERAPEUTIC CLASS: Leukotriene receptor antagonist (LTRA)

AVAILABLE FORMULATIONS:
- Rilast Tablet (10mg montelukast) — for adults and adolescents >=15 years
- Rilast Capsule (10mg montelukast) — sustained-release formulation for adults
- Rilast Syrup (4mg/5mL montelukast) — for paediatric patients aged 2-14 years

INDICATIONS:
- Prophylaxis and chronic treatment of asthma (adults and paediatric patients >= 2 years)
- Prevention of exercise-induced bronchoconstriction
- Relief of symptoms of seasonal and perennial allergic rhinitis
- Chronic asthma management as add-on therapy to inhaled corticosteroids

MECHANISM OF ACTION:
Montelukast selectively blocks the cysteinyl leukotriene receptor CysLT1, preventing leukotriene D4 from binding. This reduces bronchoconstriction, mucus secretion, vascular permeability, and eosinophil recruitment in the airways. It provides both anti-inflammatory and bronchodilatory effects.

DOSAGE AND ADMINISTRATION:
- Rilast Tablet (10mg): One tablet once daily in the evening for adults and adolescents >=15 years. Can be taken with or without food.
- Rilast Capsule (10mg): One capsule once daily in the evening. Sustained-release formulation provides steady plasma levels over 24 hours. Do not crush or chew.
- Rilast Syrup (4mg/5mL): Paediatric dosing:
  * Ages 2-5: 4mg (5mL) once daily in the evening
  * Ages 6-14: 5mg (6.25mL) once daily in the evening
  * Parents report improved breathing within 1-2 weeks of regular use

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Known hypersensitivity to montelukast or any excipient
- Not for acute asthma attacks or status asthmaticus (use rescue inhaler)
- Phenylketonuria (chewable tablets contain phenylalanine — not applicable to Rilast formulations)
- Severe hepatic impairment — use with caution

ADVERSE EFFECTS:
Common (>1%): Headache, abdominal pain, thirst
Uncommon (0.1-1%): Dizziness, drowsiness, nausea, diarrhea, rash
Rare (<0.1%): Agitation, depression, suicidal ideation (FDA boxed warning — monitor neuropsychiatric events), Churg-Strauss syndrome, elevated transaminases
Post-marketing: Dream abnormalities, insomnia, irritability

DRUG INTERACTIONS:
- CYP3A4 inducers (phenobarbital, rifampicin): May reduce montelukast levels
- CYP2C8 inhibitors (gemfibrozil): May increase montelukast levels — monitor
- Inhaled corticosteroids: Safe to use concurrently — additive benefit
- Beta-agonists: Compatible — complementary mechanism

SPECIAL POPULATIONS:
- Elderly: No dose adjustment required
- Renal impairment: No dose adjustment needed
- Hepatic impairment: Mild-moderate — no adjustment. Severe — limited data, use with caution
- Pregnancy Category B: Use only if clearly needed
- Lactation: Unknown if excreted in breast milk — use with caution

CLINICAL ADVANTAGES:
- Oral administration — no inhaler technique issues
- Once-daily dosing in the evening for optimal nighttime and morning symptom control
- Effective in both asthma and allergic rhinitis (dual benefit)
- Good safety profile in paediatric patients
- Can reduce need for rescue inhaler use
- Positive response reported in chronic asthma patients
- Parents report improved breathing in paediatric asthma cases with Rilast Syrup

STORAGE:
Tablets/Capsules: Store below 30°C. Protect from moisture and light.
Syrup: Store below 25°C. Do not refrigerate. Use within 30 days of opening.
