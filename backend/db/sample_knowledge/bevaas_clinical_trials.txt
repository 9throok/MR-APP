BEVAAS (Amlodipine) — Clinical Trial Summary
==============================================

TRIAL 1: ALLHAT (Antihypertensive and Lipid-Lowering Treatment to Prevent Heart Attack Trial)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Study Design: Randomized, double-blind, active-controlled
Patients: 33,357 high-risk hypertensive patients aged >=55 years
Duration: 4.9 years (mean follow-up)
Arms: Amlodipine vs Chlorthalidone vs Lisinopril

Key Results:
- No significant difference in primary endpoint (fatal CHD or non-fatal MI) between groups
- Amlodipine comparable to diuretic therapy for preventing major cardiovascular events
- BP reduction: Amlodipine achieved 0.8 mmHg lower systolic BP than lisinopril
- Heart failure risk slightly higher with amlodipine vs chlorthalidone (RR 1.38)
- Stroke risk: Amlodipine showed 23% lower stroke risk vs lisinopril (p=0.003)
- Conclusion: Amlodipine is effective first-line therapy for hypertension

TRIAL 2: ASCOT-BPLA (Anglo-Scandinavian Cardiac Outcomes Trial)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
Study Design: Randomized, multicenter, open-label with blinded endpoints
Patients: 19,257 hypertensive patients with >=3 cardiovascular risk factors
Duration: 5.5 years (stopped early for benefit)
Arms: Amlodipine (+/- perindopril) vs Atenolol (+/- bendroflumethiazide)

Key Results:
- Amlodipine regimen reduced all-cause mortality by 11% (p=0.025)
- Cardiovascular mortality reduced by 24% (p=0.001)
- Stroke reduced by 23% (p=0.003)
- New-onset diabetes 30% lower with amlodipine regimen (p<0.001)
- Trial stopped 18 months early because of clear superiority of amlodipine-based regimen
- Conclusion: Amlodipine-based treatment is superior for high-risk hypertensives

TRIAL 3: CAMELOT (Comparison of Amlodipine vs Enalapril to Limit Occurrences of Thrombosis)
---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Study Design: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled
Patients: 1,991 patients with angiographically documented coronary artery disease and normal BP
Duration: 2 years

Key Results:
- Amlodipine reduced cardiovascular events by 31% vs placebo (p=0.003)
- IVUS substudy showed amlodipine slowed progression of coronary atherosclerosis
- Enalapril showed a non-significant 15% reduction vs placebo
- Amlodipine reduced hospitalizations for angina by 42% (p=0.002)
- Conclusion: Amlodipine provides cardiovascular protection even in normotensive CAD patients

TRIAL 4: Zenrac-BEV-002 — Real-World Effectiveness (India)
------------------------------------------------------------
Study Design: Prospective, observational, multicenter
Patients: 1,800 Indian hypertensive patients
Duration: 24 weeks
Sites: 60 centers across India

Key Results:
- Bevaas 5mg: Mean SBP reduction of 18.2 mmHg at Week 12 (early-stage hypertension)
- Bevaas 10mg: Mean SBP reduction of 24.6 mmHg at Week 12
- Bevaas 20mg: Mean SBP reduction of 31.4 mmHg at Week 12 (resistant hypertension)
- BP goal (<140/90 mmHg) achieved in 72% of patients by Week 24
- Elderly subgroup (>65 years): Good tolerance, 68% achieved BP goal
- Combination therapy (Bevaas + ACE-I or ARB): 85% achieved BP goal
- Adverse events: Peripheral edema (8.2%), headache (3.1%), flushing (2.4%)
- Edema was dose-dependent: 4.1% (5mg), 8.9% (10mg), 14.2% (20mg)
- No serious cardiovascular adverse events attributed to treatment
- BP stabilization observed across all dose groups

SAFETY SUMMARY:
- Well-established safety profile with >30 years of global clinical use
- No increased risk of cancer (confirmed by meta-analysis of 150,000+ patients)
- Safe in diabetic patients — does not affect glucose metabolism
- Safe in patients with renal impairment
- Peripheral edema is the most common dose-limiting side effect
- Can be mitigated by combining with ACE-I or ARB (reduces edema by ~50%)
