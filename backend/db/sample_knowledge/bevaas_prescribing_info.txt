BEVAAS (Amlodipine) — Prescribing Information
===============================================

BRAND NAME: Bevaas
GENERIC NAME: Amlodipine Besylate
MANUFACTURER: Zenrac Pharmaceuticals Pvt. Ltd.
THERAPEUTIC CLASS: Calcium channel blocker (dihydropyridine)

AVAILABLE STRENGTHS:
- Bevaas 5mg tablets
- Bevaas 10mg tablets
- Bevaas 20mg tablets (for resistant hypertension)

INDICATIONS:
- Essential hypertension (first-line or add-on therapy)
- Chronic stable angina pectoris
- Vasospastic (Prinzmetal's) angina
- Resistant hypertension (as part of combination therapy)
- Early-stage hypertension in elderly patients

MECHANISM OF ACTION:
Amlodipine inhibits the transmembrane influx of calcium ions into vascular smooth muscle and cardiac muscle. It selectively inhibits L-type calcium channels, causing relaxation of coronary and peripheral arterial smooth muscle. This reduces peripheral vascular resistance (afterload), lowers blood pressure, and increases coronary blood flow. It has a gradual onset and long duration of action (24+ hours).

DOSAGE AND ADMINISTRATION:
- Bevaas 5mg: One tablet once daily. Starting dose for most patients, including elderly. Suitable for early-stage hypertension. Good tolerance observed in elderly patients.
- Bevaas 10mg: One tablet once daily. Titrate up from 5mg after 1-2 weeks if BP not adequately controlled. Effective for BP reduction.
- Bevaas 20mg: One tablet once daily. For resistant hypertension not controlled by 10mg or combination therapy. Use under specialist supervision. Effective in combination therapy for resistant hypertension. BP stabilization observed.

CONTRAINDICATIONS:
- Known hypersensitivity to amlodipine, other dihydropyridines, or any excipient
- Severe aortic stenosis
- Cardiogenic shock
- Unstable angina (except Prinzmetal's)
- Severe hypotension (systolic < 90 mmHg)
- Pregnancy and lactation

ADVERSE EFFECTS:
Common (>1%): Peripheral edema (dose-dependent, up to 10% at higher doses), headache, flushing, dizziness, fatigue
Uncommon (0.1-1%): Palpitations, nausea, abdominal pain, somnolence, muscle cramps
Rare (<0.1%): Gingival hyperplasia, gynecomastia, hepatitis, jaundice, erythema multiforme
Very rare: Myocardial infarction, arrhythmia

DRUG INTERACTIONS:
- CYP3A4 inhibitors (ketoconazole, diltiazem, erythromycin): May increase amlodipine levels — monitor BP
- CYP3A4 inducers (rifampicin, carbamazepine): May reduce amlodipine levels — may need dose increase
- Simvastatin: Limit simvastatin to 20mg/day when co-administered with amlodipine
- Cyclosporine: Amlodipine may increase cyclosporine levels — monitor
- Beta-blockers: Safe combination — complementary mechanism for BP and heart rate control
- ACE inhibitors/ARBs: Effective combination for hypertension — commonly used together
- Diuretics: Additive hypotensive effect — monitor for excessive BP lowering
- Sildenafil: Additive hypotensive effect — use with caution

SPECIAL POPULATIONS:
- Elderly: Start with 5mg. Good tolerance in elderly patients. No dose adjustment needed for age alone.
- Renal impairment: No dose adjustment required (not significantly dialyzed)
- Hepatic impairment: Start with 2.5-5mg. Reduce dose in severe impairment. Amlodipine is extensively hepatically metabolized.
- Pregnancy Category C: Contraindicated. Switch to methyldopa or labetalol.
- Lactation: Excreted in breast milk — not recommended.
- Paediatric (6-17 years): 2.5-5mg once daily for hypertension

CLINICAL TRIAL DATA:
- ALLHAT Trial: Amlodipine demonstrated comparable cardiovascular outcomes to ACE inhibitors and diuretics in high-risk hypertensive patients.
- ASCOT-BPLA: Amlodipine-based regimen showed superior cardiovascular protection versus atenolol-based regimen.
- CAMELOT Trial: Amlodipine reduced cardiovascular events in patients with coronary artery disease and normal blood pressure.
- VALUE Trial: Amlodipine provided rapid and sustained BP reduction compared to valsartan.

CLINICAL ADVANTAGES:
- Once-daily dosing with true 24-hour coverage
- Smooth, gradual onset — avoids reflex tachycardia
- Long half-life (30-50 hours) — forgiving of missed doses
- Effective as monotherapy and in combination
- Well-established safety profile with decades of clinical use
- Particularly effective for isolated systolic hypertension in elderly
- BP stabilization observed in resistant hypertension with Bevaas 20mg
- Effective in combination therapy with ACE inhibitors and diuretics

STORAGE: Store below 30°C. Protect from light and moisture.
