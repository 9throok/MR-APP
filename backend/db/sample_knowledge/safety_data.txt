ZENRAC PHARMACEUTICALS — SAFETY DATA SUMMARY
===============================================

DERISE (Desloratadine) — Safety Profile
-----------------------------------------

Adverse Event Rates (from pooled clinical trials, N=4,200):
- Headache: 6.2% (placebo 5.8%)
- Dry mouth: 3.1% (placebo 1.8%)
- Fatigue: 2.4% (placebo 2.1%)
- Somnolence: 1.2% (placebo 0.9%)
- Nausea: 1.1% (placebo 0.8%)
- Dizziness: 0.8% (placebo 0.6%)
- Myalgia: 0.5% (placebo 0.4%)
- Tachycardia: 0.1% (placebo 0.1%)

Serious Adverse Events: <0.1% — no drug-related serious adverse events in pivotal trials
Discontinuation rate due to AEs: 2.3% (vs 2.0% placebo)

Special Safety Notes:
- No QTc prolongation at doses up to 45mg (9x Derise 5mg dose)
- Safe in combination with azithromycin and ketoconazole (pharmacokinetic studies)
- No dose-dependent increase in sedation from 10mg to 50mg
- Weight-neutral: No significant weight change vs placebo over 12 weeks
- No withdrawal or rebound effects on discontinuation

Pregnancy & Lactation:
- Category B: Animal studies showed no teratogenicity. No adequate human studies.
- Excreted in breast milk in small amounts. Risk-benefit assessment required.

Overdose:
- Doses up to 45mg in clinical studies were well-tolerated
- Symptoms of overdose: Somnolence, increased heart rate
- Treatment: Supportive care, gastric lavage if recent ingestion
- Not removed by hemodialysis


RILAST (Montelukast) — Safety Profile
---------------------------------------

Adverse Event Rates (from pooled clinical trials, N=3,800):
- Headache: 18.4% (placebo 18.1%)
- Upper respiratory infection: 8.2% (not drug-related, similar to placebo)
- Abdominal pain: 2.9% (placebo 2.5%)
- Cough: 2.7% (placebo 2.4%)
- Influenza: 1.6% (placebo 1.5%)
- Diarrhea: 1.1% (placebo 0.8%)
- Rash: 0.8% (placebo 0.6%)
- Elevated ALT: 0.4% (placebo 0.3%)

Neuropsychiatric Events (post-marketing, estimated frequency):
- Sleep disturbances/vivid dreams: ~1 in 1,000
- Agitation/aggression: ~1 in 10,000
- Depression: ~1 in 10,000
- Suicidal ideation: ~1 in 100,000
- FDA Boxed Warning (March 2020): Healthcare providers should counsel patients about potential neuropsychiatric events

Serious Adverse Events:
- Churg-Strauss syndrome: Very rare, reported primarily during corticosteroid dose reduction
- Hepatic eosinophilic infiltration: Very rare
- Angioedema: Very rare

Discontinuation rate due to AEs: 3.1% (vs 2.4% placebo)

Pregnancy & Lactation:
- Category B: Animal studies showed no harm. Limited human data.
- Unknown if excreted in breast milk. Use with caution.


BEVAAS (Amlodipine) — Safety Profile
--------------------------------------

Adverse Event Rates (from pooled clinical trials, N=11,000+):
- Peripheral edema: 8.3% (placebo 0.6%) — dose-dependent
- Headache: 7.3% (placebo 7.8%)
- Fatigue/Somnolence: 4.5% (placebo 2.8%)
- Dizziness: 3.4% (placebo 1.5%)
- Flushing: 2.6% (placebo 0.3%)
- Palpitations: 1.4% (placebo 0.6%)
- Nausea: 2.9% (placebo 1.9%)
- Abdominal pain: 1.6% (placebo 0.3%)
- Muscle cramps: 1.0% (placebo 0.3%)

Edema by dose:
- 2.5mg: 1.8%
- 5mg: 4.1%
- 10mg: 8.9%
- 20mg: 14.2%

Serious Adverse Events:
- Severe hypotension: Rare, primarily in patients with severe aortic stenosis
- Acute pulmonary edema: Very rare
- Hepatitis/jaundice: Very rare
- Gingival hyperplasia: Rare (~0.1%), reversible on discontinuation
- Gynecomastia: Very rare

Discontinuation rate due to AEs: 3.8% (vs 1.9% placebo)

Pregnancy & Lactation:
- Category C: Animal studies showed delayed parturition and prolonged labor. No adequate human studies. Contraindicated.
- Excreted in breast milk. Not recommended during breastfeeding.

Overdose:
- Symptoms: Excessive peripheral vasodilation, marked hypotension, reflex tachycardia
- Treatment: IV calcium gluconate, vasopressors, fluids, activated charcoal (if within 1 hour)
- Hemodialysis not effective (amlodipine is highly protein-bound)
- Reported survival after ingestion of 70mg with supportive care

Drug Safety in Special Populations:
- Diabetics: Metabolically neutral. No effect on HbA1c, fasting glucose, or lipids.
- CKD patients: Safe to use. No dose adjustment for mild-moderate renal impairment.
- Heart failure: Safe in HFrEF (PRAISE trial). Avoid in acute decompensated HF.
- Post-MI: Safe to initiate amlodipine post-myocardial infarction (CAMELOT data).
